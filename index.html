Health and Wellness Talking Points
• Our Health and Wellness Program is available to all residents and is a great way to maintain the progress made during skilled therapy.

• Sessions are $55 for 30 minutes, with no minimum or maximum commitment.

• Most residents participate twice per week, though the schedule is completely flexible.

• The program is designed to support continued strength, balance, and mobility in a consistent, supportive setting.

• Health and Wellness is a private pay service and does not go through insurance, so there are no visit limits or authorization requirements.

• Residents can start or stop at any time. There is no contract or obligation.
Insurance Visit Preservation Language
When explaining to a family why therapy is ending before all visits are used:

• "We are intentionally wrapping up skilled services at this time in order to preserve the remaining visits. Should a change in condition or a new need arise later in the year, those visits will be available and ready to use."

• "Insurance authorizes a set number of visits per calendar year. Rather than using all of them now, we are holding the remaining visits in reserve so they are there if needed down the road."

• "This is something we do to advocate for our residents. Protecting those visits means your loved one has a safety net if something comes up later."

When a family pushes back on ending therapy:

• "I understand the concern. The good news is that we are not taking anything away. Those visits remain available, and if there is ever a new skilled need, we can get therapy started again quickly."

• "Our Health and Wellness Program is a great option for continuing to work on strength and mobility in the meantime, without using any insurance visits."
Common Family Questions
Why is therapy only twice a week?
"The frequency is based on what our clinical team has determined to be the most appropriate dosage for your loved one's current needs. We want to make sure each session is productive and that there is adequate time for rest and recovery between visits."
Why is my loved one being discharged from therapy?
"Skilled therapy requires that we demonstrate measurable functional improvement. When a resident has met their goals, reached a stable level, or when continuing to bill insurance is no longer clinically appropriate, we transition them out of skilled services. This protects their remaining visits for future use."
What is the difference between skilled therapy and Health and Wellness?
"Skilled therapy is covered by insurance and requires a medical need with measurable goals. Health and Wellness is a private pay program that allows residents to continue working on strength, balance, and mobility without the restrictions of insurance. It is $55 for a 30 minute session with no visit limits."
Can my loved one get more therapy visits?
"The number of visits is determined by the insurance authorization. If there is a change in condition or a new skilled need, we can evaluate for additional services. In the meantime, our Health and Wellness Program is available for ongoing support without using insurance visits."
Why are you not using all of the authorized visits?
"We are preserving the remaining visits so they are available later in the year if your loved one experiences a change in condition or a new need arises. This is something we do to protect their benefits."
What happens after therapy ends?
"Before wrapping up, your loved one will receive a Home Exercise Program to continue working on their goals independently. We also offer a Health and Wellness Program for residents who want continued support from our therapy team. Sessions are $55 for 30 minutes with no minimum or maximum commitment."
